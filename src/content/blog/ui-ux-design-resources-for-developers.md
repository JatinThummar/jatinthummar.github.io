You don't need a design degree to build beautiful, usable interfaces. These are the resources I've found most useful for learning UI/UX — from theory and inspiration to hands-on tools and practice challenges.

## Courses & Learning Platforms

- **[Google UX Design Course](https://grow.google/intl/en_in/ux-design-course/)** — Google's professional certificate on Coursera. Covers the full UX process from research to prototyping.
- **[Interaction Design Foundation](https://www.interaction-design.org/)** — One of the most comprehensive UX education platforms. Courses on everything from usability to design thinking.
- **[Hack Design](https://hackdesign.org/lessons)** — Free design lessons curated by top designers, delivered weekly.
- **[IBM Design Thinking](https://www.ibm.com/design/thinking/page/courses/Practitioner)** — IBM's free practitioner course on applying design thinking at scale.
- **[HPI Design Thinking](https://open.hpi.de/courses?q=&channel=design-thinking)** — Free online courses on design thinking from Hasso Plattner Institute.
- **[Baseline HQ](https://www.baselinehq.com/course.html)** — Typography-focused design course. Essential for getting text right.
- **[10k Designers](https://www.10kdesigners.com/)** — Indian design school for UI/UX. Cohort-based, project-driven.
- **[UXPin Ebooks](https://www.uxpin.com/studio/ebooks/)** — Free ebooks on UX design, prototyping, and design systems.
- **[BiteSize UX](https://bitesizeux.com/)** — Short, digestible UX lessons for busy people.
- **[Simplified UX](https://simplifiedux.com/)** — UX concepts explained simply with visual examples.
- **[Egghead](https://egghead.io/)** — Expert-led courses for front-end web developers. Short, focused screencasts.
- **[Kickass UX](https://www.kickassux.com/)** — UX education for creatives. Practical, no-fluff approach.
- **[Design Better](https://www.designbetter.co/)** — Discover the world's best design practices. Long-form guides from InVision.

## UX Theory & Principles

- **[Laws of UX](https://lawsofux.com/)** — The essential psychological principles that every designer should know. Beautifully presented.
- **[Principles.design](https://principles.design/)** — Open source collection of design principles from teams around the world.
- **[NN Group](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/)** — The gold standard for evidence-based UX research and guidelines.
- **[NN Group — Psychology & UX](https://www.nngroup.com/topic/psychology-and-ux/)** — How cognitive psychology shapes user behavior and design decisions.
- **[NN Group — UX Process](https://www.nngroup.com/topic/ux-design-process/)** — Practical guidance on running a UX design process.
- **[Farnam Street — Mental Models](https://fs.blog/mental-models/)** — Not design-specific, but the mental models here apply directly to product thinking.
- **[Psychology of Design — 106 Cognitive Biases](https://growth.design/psychology#hicks-law)** — Visual reference of cognitive biases that affect design decisions.

- **[Apple Human Interface Guidelines](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/guidelines/overview/)** — Apple's design bible. Required reading for iOS and macOS design.
- **[Shopify Polaris](https://polaris.shopify.com/)** — One of the best-documented design systems. Great for learning how to structure components.
- **[Carbon Design System](https://carbondesignsystem.com/)** — IBM's open-source design system. Thorough and accessible.
- **[Lightning Design System](https://www.lightningdesignsystem.com/)** — Salesforce's enterprise design system.
- **[CRED NeoPop](https://cred.club/neopop)** — CRED's bold, 3D-inspired design system.
- **[Spotify Design](https://spotify.design/article/reimagining-design-systems-at-spotify)** — How Spotify reimagined their design system at scale.
- **[Design Systems Surf](https://designsystems.surf/)** — Curated collection of design systems from real companies.
- **[Design Systems Repo](https://designsystemsrepo.com/design-systems/)** — Searchable directory of open-source design systems.
- **[The Design System Guide](https://thedesignsystem.guide/)** — Comprehensive guide to building and maintaining design systems.
- **[DesignSystems.com](https://www.designsystems.com/)** — Articles, talks, and resources about design systems from Figma.
- **[What is a Design System?](https://backlight.dev/mastery/what-is-a-design-system)** — In-depth explainer on design system fundamentals from Backlight.
- **[Style Guides & Tools](http://styleguides.io/tools.html)** — Curated collection of style guide tools and resources.
- **[Branding Style Guides Archive](https://brandingstyleguides.com/)** — Real brand guideline documents from major companies.

- **[Miro](https://miro.com/)** — Collaborative whiteboarding for brainstorming, user flows, and workshops.
- **[Notion](https://www.notion.so/)** — Not a design tool, but essential for design documentation and specs.

- **[Popmotion](https://popmotion.io/)** — The animator's JavaScript toolbox. Functional, flexible animation library.
